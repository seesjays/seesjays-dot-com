I’ve been watching _Doctor Who_ recently to brush up on a craft I’ll discuss later. I’m two seasons in so far, and the only question on my mind is how they extended this to _eight seasons._ Four would be enough, methinks. Five is pushing it, but to barge past sensibility and make eight? Astounding.

I hope the show’s length is justified, and not just them overusing a premise that I think would be suited for five seasons absolute max. It’s not that I don’t like the show, I actually love it lots. But I don’t like things that are strained from being lengthened. Shows are great when they end when they need to, not when they exist in perpetuity.

But I’d be kidding myself if I said Doctor Who couldn’t be justified to last eight seasons. I’m sure they’ll all be great, and the whole immortality schtick really helps to keep it going. What truly _doesn’t_ deserve to exist, however is _Grey’s Anatomy._

**NINETEEN** seasons of hospital drama. I’ve seen four episodes out of four hundred and honestly find myself questioning how they could stretch it that far.

Alas, my media opinions are unguided. I don’t watch much TV.
